What is ESG?

The Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria help to better determine the future financial performance of companies (return and risk).

Calculate ESG Rating

Theory Behind ESG Scores

Environmental Impact: The Environmental Score evaluates a company’s commitment to preserving natural resources, managing waste, reducing emissions, and utilizing renewable energy. It reflects a company’s responsibility towards mitigating climate change and environmental degradation.

Social Responsibility: The Social Score highlights a company’s impact on its employees, customers, and communities. It measures factors like employee satisfaction, community engagement, health and safety, and workforce diversity.

Governance Policies: The Governance Score measures a company’s adherence to ethical business practices, transparency, and strong leadership. It focuses on board diversity, executive compensation, anti-corruption policies, and shareholder rights.
